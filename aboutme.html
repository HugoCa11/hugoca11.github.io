This is who I am

Computer Science graduate and experienced game developer with a strong background in VR, interactive applications, and game development. Proficient in designing and integrating engaging user experiences, collaborating with multidisciplinary teams, and implementing innovative solutions. Additionally, skilled in Salesforce development, contributing to large-scale projects in the financial industry. Passionate about technology, problem-solving, and bringing creative ideas to life.

My Story

Shaped by Change

Born in one of the world's largest cities, Mexico City, I’ve had the incredible opportunity to live in 14 different cities across four countries—Mexico, Argentina, Guatemala, and Canada—mainly due to my father’s job. Each move has allowed me to immerse myself in different cultures, adapt to new environments, and embrace change with confidence. Through these experiences, I have developed the ability to learn quickly, navigate diverse situations, and see change not as a challenge but as an opportunity for growth.

My Interests

Technology

Technology has always been a driving force in my life, shaping both my personal growth and professional career. From an early age, my curiosity for innovation ed me to explore different areas of development, from game creation and interactive experiences to enterprise solutions like Salesforce. Working on VR games, interactive applications, and large-scale financial industry projects has given me a deep understanding of how technology can enhance experiences and solve complex problems. Constantly adapting to new tools, frameworks, and methodologies, I see technology not just as a career path but as an ever-evolving landscape where creativity and problem-solving meet.

Sports

Sports have always been a big part of my life, both as a player and a fan. I’m especially passionate about football (soccer), American football, hockey, and Formula 1. Over the years, I’ve played in various teams, most recently during my time at Softtek and in my last year at university. I also had the opportunity to be part of the ITESM Campus Morelia volleyball team for three semesters. Beyond the competition, sports have taught me invaluable lessons—most importantly, the power of teamwork, adaptability, and strategy.

Music

Music has always been an essential part of my life, both as a listener and a creator. I enjoy a wide range of genres, from rock and electronic to classical and jazz. Over the years, I’ve played a few instruments and even collaborated with others in small projects. Music has taught me a lot about discipline, creativity, and collaboration—skills that have translated into my professional life. Just as in music, I believe that in development, every element must work together in harmony, and innovation often comes from combining different influences and perspectives.

Social Service

Memory and Tolerance Museum (Museo Memoria y Tolerancia)

One of my Social Service experiences was at the Museo Memoria y Tolerancia in Mexico City. Usually the main activities for the students consisted of helping the visitors. But my case was different due to my background I was assigned to help on the maintenance and configuration of computer equipment of the museum and the offices and also help in the configuration and installation of audio guides.

English Teacher - ITESM Community Learning Center

Other of my Social Service experiences was as an English teacher for adults at the Community Learning Center of the ITESM Campus Santa Fe. Basically the activities consisted on teach the basics to adults (+18) that had none previous experience with the language.

Informatics Teacher - ITESM Community Learning Center

In the same community center I also participated as informatics teacher. In this case the activities consisted on teaching the basics of informatics such as teach the difference between software and hardware, how to use Micosoft Office, etc.
